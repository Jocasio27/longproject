Major Titles Won

PBA Elite Players Championship 2011

South Point Bowling Center in Las Vegas, Nev., Nov. 20, 2011. The top four qualifiers bowled in a one game match, lowest score gets elimintated. Belmonte stayed in the fight with a 237 game. Norm Duke was the odd man out shooting only a 222. Game two: Belmo shot a 205, just edging out Mike Scroggins with a 190. In the Championship match, Belmote defeated Mike DeVaney 255-238. Earning Belmonte his first Major title and a prize totaling $35,000.

USBC Masters 2013

Brunswick Zone Carolier, North Brunswick, N.Y. As the number one qualifier, Belmonte ony had to bowl one game to claim the tournament win. In the championship match Belmonte took on Wes Malott, and won with a final score of 258-245. A true battle of the titans. This title net him his sceond major and a prize of $50,000.

PBA Tournament of Champions 2014

Thunderbowl Lanes,Allen Park, Michigan. Another number one qulaifer, big suprise there. One game to decide who wins the title, Malott vs. Belmonte. Belmonte takes the 2014 Tournament of Champions with a nailbiter of a finish, 219-218

USBC Masters 2014

Brunswick Zone Carolier, North Brunswick, NJ. Coming in as the number 5 qualifer, Belmonte has to run the ladder to secure the title. In match one he takes on Ryan Ciminelli and takes him down 227-214. In the next match Belmonte takes on the number 3 seed Tom Smallwood. In a twenty nine pin win, Belmonte wins 202-173. For the semi-final match Belmo takes on Michael Haugen Jr. Haugen struck twice in the 10th, leaving a stone 7 on his third shot finishing with a game of 234. But Belmonte comes in clutch shooting an 8 spare in the first part of the 10th, and shooting nine in the fill frame to win the match 235-234. Now for the championship match. 1 seed vs. 5 seed. Belmo Vs. Tackett. Belmonte closes it out 221-177.

USBC Masters 2015

Ashwaubenon Bowling Alley, Green Bay, WI. As the number 2 qualifier, he automatically bowls in the semi-final match, where he takes on Hall of Famer Pete Webber. Belmote moves on to the finals 232-210. In the finals, Belmonte faces an amature bowler A.J. Johnson. It was a close match all the way to 8th frame. But the amature fell apart come closing time. Allowing Belmote to take home his 5th major title 202-157.

PBA Tournament of Champions 2015

Woodland Bowl, Indianapolis, IN. Another number 2 qualifier. In the semi-finals Belmonte faces Sean Rash for a spot in the championship match. With a comeback win, Belmonte moves on to face Rhino Page. And in a shutout fashion he wins 232-214.

PBA Players Championship 2017

After a two year dryspell of major wins, Belmonte has a chance to claim his 7th major title. Coming in as the number one qualifer Belmonte takes on Anthony Simonsen in the final match. For his 7th major title, Belmonte takes the win 221-204.

USBC Masters 2017

Coming off the dryspell, Belmonte now has a chance to take home his second major in 2017. With another top seed, he takes on Michael Tang. Almost shooting a perfect game, Belmo takes home yet another major title 272-212.

PBA World Championship 2017

For a feat that has never been done, Belmote has the opportunity to be the first to take home 3 majors in one year, it wont be easy, but he did qualify as the number 2 seed. In the semi-finals he takes on Ryan Ciminelli. For the first time in his major title career, he goes into a roll off. Throwing a nine count, it came down to Ciminelli throwing 8 or less to move on to the finals, and he leaves a seven count! In the finals match, Belmonte takes on Jesper Svensson, another two hander. Svensson finished spare-strike to finish with 225, forcing Belmo to strike and 9 count in the tenth to make history. With the second shot in the tenth, Belmonte strikes, winning the match, and claiming his third major title in one year, cementing his place in PBA history.

PBA Tournament of Champions 2019

With a chance at 10 major titles, Belmonte has qualified first with a free ride to the championship match. The finals match would be between Tackett and Belmonte, which has become quite the rivalry. But Tackett was no match for Belmo this time around. And for his 10th career major title, Belmonte wins the 2019 Tournament of Champions Matched only by hall of famers, Earl Anthony and Pete Webber, and it only took him 12 years, compared to Anthony's 16, and Webber's 35 years.

PBA World Championship 2019

With yet another top seed Belmonte has a shot to take home another major title. In the finals Belmote takes on another powerhouse two hander in Jakob Butturff. Butturff struck in his last 6 balls, striking three times in the tenth, forcing Belmo to strike-spare in the tenth to become the all time leader in major titles. With his second shot in the 10th, Belmonte strikes, taking home the win, and not only claiming his 11th major title, but also earning him the title of GOAT.

U.S. Open 2020

The one major that has eluded Belmonte, the U.S. Open. Belmonte has never won the Open, but that will change in the 2020 Open. As the number 2 seed, he takes on Dick Allen in the semi-final match, and wins 215-187 to move on to the Championship match against the youngest bowler to ever win the U.S. Open, Anthony Simonsen. Both bolwers had their struggles, as they both had relatively low scores. Belmonte needed either a strike or a spare in the 10th to shut out Simonsen, and with his first ball he threw a 6 count. To no shock to anyone, he converts the spare to win his 12th major title, further cementing his phenominal career. This win also secured his "super slam", which is winning all five major titles at least once, becoming the second ever to do so.

PBA World Championship 2020

With covid-19 breaking out during this time, this was one of the last events before the major outbreak. But as the number one qualifer, Belmonte is automatically into the finals, where he takes on Anthony Simonsen. Belmonte was dominant throughout the match, and ultimately won 213-190, securing his third World Championship, tying him for 2nd most with Norm Duke.

PBA Players Championship 2022

The PBA Players Championship was the first major of the 2022 season, and Belmonte has the opportunity to claim major title number 14. Belmo qualified numbeer two, to take on Arturo Quintero who qualified 3rd. In Belmo fashion he struck out in the tenth frame to secure the win, moving on to the championsip match agains Sean Rash. With a fairly slow start, Rash was behind from the begining, making it tough to come back. Coming into the tenth Belmonte needed a strike with his first ball to ensure the Rash couldn't shut him out. He makes a ball change and strikes with his first ball, and shoots 9 with his second, securing his 14th major title, his first since begining of Covid, extending his major title record.
